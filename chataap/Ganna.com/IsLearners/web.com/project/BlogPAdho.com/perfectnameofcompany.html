7 steps to the perfect name for your company

When starting a company, coming up with a good company name is part of the process and preferably one that makes
an immediate impression and sticks with customers.But how do you choose a good name for your company and what
do you have to take into account?

Come up with a clear company name

Whether you build websites or run a bakery; make sure your name clearly shows what product or service you offer.You can
do this simply by literally adding your ‘species name’ to your company name. It immediately becomes clear to customers
what you offer or sell. “Make sure you have a name that says exactly what you do or doesn’t do it at all, so
that you have to explain the connection. Something that is in between, it is often just not.”

Provide creativity

A striking name sticks with customers, that makes you easier to find on the internet. How original your name can be, does
depend on the activities of your company. A broker will be less playful with his company name than a layout and
design agency. Being creative on command is quite difficult for many entrepreneurs. So start sparring with other
entrepreneurs, organize a contest via social media or hire a professional.

Avoid duplication

Are you not a star at coming up with a name and do you think you can make it easy on yourself by being inspired by
other names? Be careful, your company name should not be too similar to an existing one. If you call your
neighborhood supermarket ‘C2000’, for example, you can expect objections.You can check whether your name
resembles a trademark in the trademark register of the BOIP (Benelux Office for Intellectual Property). Also keep in mind
that your name should not be misleading. Are you self-employed, but do you call your company Samson by? Then
this can cause problems.

Don’t make your company name too funny

Don’t confuse creativity with humors. Don’t make your company's name helarious, because a ‘too’ funny company
name can create the idea that you don't ever deal with your profession professionally, that you don't take the assignments
too seriously.

Don’t make your name too specific

Avoid framing your business activities too much with your name, otherwise you will never be asked for other services. With
a broader name you also reach a larger target group. When you start as a freelance marketer, you can also specialize
in SEO copywriting in the future. A name like Janssen marketing can then be too restrictive.

Test your name

Happy with the name you came up with? Test if others think he is as brilliant as you are. Ask family members and
friends, but also entrepreneurs from your network, what association they have when hearing the name. Take their feedback
seriously because if they have doubts, your future customers will too.

Check your name

Check your name in the Trade Register . You may not choose a name that someone else already has. I experienced this with
my company name Padho. “That name has already been forgotten. We decided to make it BlogPadho. At first
we weren’t happy with the name change, but after hearing it a lot, I started to like it more and more.” Also check the
availability of your intended domain name. It’s a shame if you’ve found a good name, but can’t link a suitable URL to it.
