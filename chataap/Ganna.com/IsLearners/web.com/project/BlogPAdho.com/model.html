Do you want to promote your service offers on the internet but do not have a sales team? Affiliate marketing the
solution you need.

AFFILIATE DEFINITION

Affiliate marketing is quite similar to a network of business introducers: partners recommend your offers on your behalf
and take a commission on each sale they bring to you.

It is therefore a system that works on performance, you will pay your partners only when you receive a sale or a
remunerative action.

This economic model was implemented on the Internet for the first time by Jeff Bezos, founder of Amazon, who
imagined this economic model in 1996. It then arrived in France in the 2000s.

Affiliate marketing is particularly used by major e-commerce brands such as Fnac, Darty or C-Discount, and more
particularly in the digital product sector.

The affiliation model is not only reserved for web giants, any freelancer, whatever his activity, can choose
this lever to find customers and boost his sales.

THE DIFFERENT STAKEHOLDERS

Affiliate marketing involves three actors:


  • The Affiliate : He has a website that attracts an audience and promotes an offer to its visitors. With each sale
    made, he will receive a commission, a percentage of the price paid by the end buyer.
  • The Seller : He seeks to sell a product or service and registers on an affiliate platform to find one or more partners
    who will promote it. With each sale that his partner brings him, he pays him a commission.
  • The Buye : By browsing the affiliate’s site, he sees that he is recommending the product or service of another
    person (the seller). He clicks on the provided link and orders the seller’s offer. Unknowingly, it just made money
    for the affiliate, who took a percentage of the price.

    • AFFILIATE MARKETING COMPENSATION MODELS

    Cost per Action (CPA)

    The CPA model is the most popular and beneficial compensation model for advertisers.
    It allows them to only pay for a specific user action, such as a purchase, subscription or registration.
    He thus reduces the risk as much as possible because he does not pay for traffic that does not convert and can therefore increase his marketing budget.

    Cost per Sale (CPS)

    It’s a popular compensation model in the affiliate world.
    The affiliate pays a commission to the seller for each sale generated.

    There are two types of compensation:

    Fixed-rate remuneration: the affiliate pays a pre-determined sum to the seller for each sale generated from his site.

    Variable rate remuneration: the affiliate determines a commission percentage based on the sale price (10% of sales for example).
    Compared to fixed rates, percentage rates are often used for high-priced products (eg, cars).

    Cost per Lead (CPL)

    This method of remuneration allows payment for each contact generated, such as a subscription to a newsletter or the filling of a form on the site.

    In the pay-per-lead model, the affiliate will only pay for valid leads that can later be converted into sales by the company.

    AFFILIATE PLATFORM AND COOKIES

    To set up an affiliate marketing program, the seller generally goes through an affiliate platform a technical solution to manage the sales
    generated by its affiliates.

    To tailor offers to the tastes of site visitors, a system of cookies is usually used, text files that are stored by the web browser on the hard driveof a website
    visitor. These files are used to record information on the visitor or on his journey through the site.
